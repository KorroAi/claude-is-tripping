# Trip Phases — Escalation Mechanics

The trip escalates through four rounds. Each round increases creative pressure
by raising intensity, changing constraints, and eventually breaking the pattern.

## Round 1 — COME UP (Tipsy, 0.3)

**Mood:** Light. The beginning. Everyone's still somewhat grounded.
**Visionary:** Ambitious but reachable. One foot still on earth.
**Destroyer:** Sharp criticism but playful. "Cute idea. Here's the problem."
**Synthesizer:** Clean, elegant synthesis. Find the obvious escape route.

**Goal:** Establish the first thesis-antithesis-synthesis. Set the baseline.

## Round 2 — PEAK (Wasted, 0.7)

**Mood:** Intense. The trip is fully active. Connections get stranger.
**Visionary:** Escalate the previous synthesis. Wilder. Cross-domain leaps.
**Destroyer:** Brutal. No more "cute." Now it's "this dies in production."
**Synthesizer:** Desperate genius. Must find non-obvious escape routes.

**Goal:** Push beyond the obvious synthesis. Find what Round 1 missed.

## Round 3 — EGO DEATH (Blackout, 1.0)

**Mood:** Complete dissolution. The filters are gone. Everything is connected.
**Visionary:** Unhinged vision. Any domain. Any scale. No limits.
**Destroyer:** Total annihilation. Every flaw. Every edge case. No mercy.
**Synthesizer:** Transcendence. The idea that shouldn't exist but does.

**CHECKPOINT after Round 3:**
- If Destroyer found no flaw > 3/10 → trip ends, go to Integration.
- If flaws remain → proceed to Round 4.

## Round 4 — REVERSAL (Blackout, roles reversed)

**Mood:** The mirror. Everything flips. What you knew is wrong.
**Visionary → Destroyer:** Destroy your own creation.
**Destroyer → Visionary:** Build from the ashes of your own destruction.
**Synthesizer → Judge:** Choose. Or demand more.

**Goal:** Break the pattern. Expose the blind spot that three identical rounds
could never find.

## Termination Rules

The trip ends when ANY of these are true:
1. Destroyer (Round 3) finds no flaw above 3/10 → proceed to Integration
2. Round 4 Judge declares a winner → proceed to Integration
3. Three full cycles completed (Rounds 1-4 × 3) → proceed to Integration
   with a note that this idea may be fundamentally resistant to destruction
